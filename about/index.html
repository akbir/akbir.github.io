about me

Hi, I’m Akbir. I am an artificial intelligence (AI) PhD candidate at UCL, advised by Tim Rocktäschel and Edward Grefenstette and affiliated with UCL DARK lab.

I’m interested in studying techniques to ensure cooperative AI. I consider these problems primarily in the setting of multi-agent deep reinforcement learning.

My research is shaped by my ML deployments and as proxy, my AI safety concerns. As a Senior Researcher at Tractable, I’ve built vision models for car apprasial. Before this, I was co-founder at Spherical Defence Labs, using language models for network intrusion detection systems. These systems are active learners, interacting in the real world now.

I received my MPhil in Computer Science from the University of Cambridge, where I had the pleasure of working with Hatice Gunes. For my undergraduate, I studied Mathematics and Physics, spending a year abroad at the University Of Toronto. For a more in-depth account, see my resume.

I’m more than just my research, feel free to explore my site and see what excites me.
